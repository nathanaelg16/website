One thing I've always wondered is how come so many people adopt the Republican way of thinking when all the GOP does is cater to themselves and the rich. The upper class is only 1% of the American population, yet almost 50% of Americans voted for Donald Trump in 2020. Every issue that Democrats and Progressives wish to resolve is not just for their own personal gain - it's for the welfare of the entire nation. Medicare For All would guarantee health coverage for <b>everyone</b>, no matter what job you have, how much money you earn, or what your condition is. The Green New Deal is about improving the environment for _our_ benefit. Raising the minimum wage, which has not kept up with inflation at all, is about allowing working-class Americans to earn a living wage. More money in our pockets means more money to spend in the economy, which means more profits for business owners. Social Security is about giving working class people a chance to retire. All of these policies are for improving the lives of the working class people, which are 99% of the population.

I understand maybe why the ultra-rich would not want any of these policies. They're entitled hypocrites. But why are common people like you and me against these things? Why would the average, working class American vote Republican, despite them not doing anything for their benefit?

For a long time I pondered over this. I thought it was because of racism. "White Americans look down on the poor, the immigrants, and the minorities because they have a superiority complex, and so it makes sense that they wouldn't want any of these policies that improve our lives," I thought. But while racism _does_ exist, it can't be the sole reason - there must exist non-racist White Americans, and I know several non-White folk who are Republicans. The truth is, they're brainwashed. They've fallen victim of a "false consciousness" propagated by the news sources they tend to read and listen to.
