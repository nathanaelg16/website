Not everyone likes science or is as devoted to it as I am, that much is true, but is that a valid excuse for not knowing the basic properties of our solar system? Are basic facts like "the Sun is a star", "the Earth is a planet", and "the Moon is a satellite" not important in our society? I can understand some confusion with whether Pluto is a planet or not. I mean, I'm pretty sure I was taught there were 9 planets in our solar system as a child and was completely perplexed when years later I learned there were in fact only 8. Older folk might reasonably still believe that there are 9. However, confusion about the nature of our Sun and Moon truly baffles me. This leads me wonder: has science education failed our society? How can we do better? Similarly, what can we do to get more kids excited about science? I don't know what TV shows are out there for kids these days, but we desperately need a replacement on par with or better than my generation's _The Magic School Bus_ and _Cyberkids_.
