## Neuro-inspired Machine Learning
### Advisor: Constantine Dovrolis

We address the issue of continual learning and controlled forgetting in deep neural networks by 
using elements of neuroscience and human cognition as inspiration. We also use methods from 
network science to study the topology of our networks. We believe that the solution to artificial 
general intelligence will be neuroinspired.

## Deflagration of Supernovae
### Advisor: Alan Calder
[Poster](https://you.stonybrook.edu/2021virtualsummersymposium/2020-ureca-virtual-posters/dcd_nathanael-gutierrez/) | [APS](https://meetings.aps.org/Meeting/APR22/Session/X13.5)

We explored how the initial conditions of Type Iax supernovae affect the amount of radioactive 
gas produced. We performed simulations with FLASH and analyzed the data with Python. 
Due to the massive amounts of data produced by our simulations, we employed the use MPI4PY to 
parallelize our analysis scripts and achieved a 40x improvement in runtime.

## Quantum Machine Learning
### Advisor: Ying Mao

We explored the development of machine learning algorithms on quantum computers. We 
used the Qiskit SDK to develop a convolutional neural network to run on IBM 
quantum computers. Since quantum computers allow data to be encoded as a 
superposition of binary states, data can be encoded much more optimally on 
a quantum computer than a classical one. We can then leverage this to 
create more powerful and more efficient machine learning systems.
